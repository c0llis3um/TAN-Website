What is blockchain and cryptocurrency?

Blockchain is a public electronic ledger -- similar to a relational database -- that can be openly shared among disparate users and that creates an unchangeable record of their transactions, each one time-stamped and linked to the previous one.

Each digital record or transaction in the thread is called a block (hence the name), and it allows either an open or controlled set of users to participate in the electronic ledger. Each block is linked to a specific participant.

Blockchain can only be updated by consensus between participants in the system, and when new data is entered, it can never be erased. The blockchain contains a true and verifiable record of each and every transaction ever made in the system.

What is a wallet?

A cryptocurrency wallet is a device, physical medium, program or a service which stores the public and/or private keys for cryptocurrency transactions. Is like a bank account where you store your digital coins.

What is a seed phrase?

A seed phrase is a series of words generated by your cryptocurrency wallet that give you access to the crypto associated with that wallet. Think of a wallet as being similar to a password manager for crypto, and the seed phrase as being like the master password. Always keep your seed safe, this is how you can access to the blockchain and recover wallets.

Decentralized finance, or DeFi, uses emerging technology to remove third parties and centralized institutions from financial transactions. The components of DeFi are cryptocurrencies, blockchain technology, and software that allow people to transact financially with each other.

Defi

DeFi stands for "Decentralized Finance," and it refers to a set of financial services and applications built on blockchain technology, particularly on public blockchains like Ethereum. The main idea behind DeFi is to recreate traditional financial systems, such as banking, lending, and trading, using decentralized and open-source protocols. This allows users to access financial services without the need for traditional intermediaries like banks, brokers, or other centralized institutions.

Key features and components of DeFi include:

1. **Smart Contracts:** DeFi platforms are often powered by smart contracts, which are self-executing contracts with the terms directly written into code. These smart contracts automate and enforce the rules of financial transactions, eliminating the need for intermediaries.

2. **Decentralized Exchanges (DEX):** DeFi platforms often include decentralized exchanges where users can trade various cryptocurrencies directly with each other, without relying on a central authority to facilitate the transactions.

3. **Lending and Borrowing:** DeFi allows users to lend their cryptocurrency assets to others in exchange for interest or borrow assets by providing collateral. Smart contracts automatically manage loan terms, interest rates, and collateralization ratios.

4. **Stablecoins:** Stablecoins are cryptocurrencies pegged to the value of traditional fiat currencies like the US Dollar. They provide a stable value and are often used within DeFi protocols to reduce exposure to the volatility of other cryptocurrencies.

5. **Yield Farming and Liquidity Mining:** Users can earn additional tokens by participating in DeFi platforms through yield farming and liquidity mining. These involve providing liquidity to decentralized exchanges or lending platforms and earning rewards in return.

6. **Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs):** DeFi projects often use DAOs for governance, allowing token holders to have a say in the decision-making process regarding protocol upgrades, changes, or other important matters.

7. **Interoperability:** DeFi applications can often interact with each other, creating a more interconnected and interoperable financial ecosystem. This allows users to seamlessly move assets and data between different DeFi platforms.

While DeFi has gained significant attention for its potential to provide financial services in a more inclusive and decentralized manner, it is important to note that the space is still relatively young and comes with certain risks, including smart contract vulnerabilities, market volatility, and regulatory uncertainties. As the DeFi ecosystem continues to evolve, it is expected to influence the broader financial industry.
